February 13, 2015 - Amsterdam

NG-NL

The NG-NL Conference in Amsterdam is the first AngularJS Conference that will take place in The Netherlands. One full day we will be hosting sessions and workshops about AngularJS and related topics.

All sessions will be in English and the conference will be from 9am to 5pm (doors will open earlier for registration). All Early Bird tickets are sold out! Regular tickets are available now. Please follow us on twitter at @ngnlconf to stay up to date.

Call for Papers

NG-NL has now closed it’s Call for Papers. We will soon announce the schedule with all the speakers and sessions.

Sales of a limited number of early bird tickets start on the 6th of December.

Speakers

More speakers will be announced soon...

Maurice de Beijer

Maurice de Beijer is an independent software consultant and trainer specializing in HTML5, REST, JavaScript, AngularJS and ASP.NET MVC. For DevelopMentor he currently teaches Essential and Advanced AngularJS, Essential HTML5, Essential jQuery, Modern Web Development and Essential ASP.NET MVC courses. He has been awarded the yearly Microsoft Most Valuable Professional award since 2005. Maurice is also very active in the Dutch dotNed user group where he regularly organizes meetings and the dotNed-Pluralsight study group meetings.

Christoph Burgdor

Christoph began with programming at the age of 10 with BASIC but has since moved on to become proficient in various different programming languages and technologies.
Christoph has contributed to many projects, including AngularJS, jquery-ui, TodoMVC and the banshee media player and is also the creator of the nickel.rs framework. When he's not evangelizing Git, he likes to travel the world with his bicycle.

Olivier Combe

Self-learning web developer, Olivier Combe loves everything front-end related and especially Javascript & CSS. His latest crush has been Angular for the last 2 years. He firmly believes in open-source and has been contributing to many projects on his free time.

Kent C. Dodds

I am Kent C. Dodds. I work at Alianza as the frontend developer working with AngularJS primarily. I co-host Angular Air, a video podcast about AngularJS, with my friend and GDE Todd Motto. I'm an Egghead.io instructor. I'm happily married and happily the father of two kids. I like code. I care about craft, design, and architecture. I like to talk about it. Come chat with me :-)

Aaron Frost

Aaron is part of Domo's crackshot Frontend Dev team. He was nominated by Google to be a Google Developer Expert for AngularJS. He is a panelist on JavaScript Jabber and Adventures in Angular. Aaron is also working on an ES6 book with O'Reilly Media. He is an accomplished speaker, having presented all around the world. He is also one of the organizers of ng-conf, the Angular community's largest developer conference of the year. His greatest accomplishment of all, however, is his beautiful family, including his wife and four children.

Gert Hengeveld

I'm a Full Stack Developer at Xebia, working with Scala, Akka and AngularJS. I'm the author of several popular articles on AngularJS (https://medium.com/opinionated-angularjs) and regularly speak at meetups. Angular has been my weapon of choice for the past 2.5 years, with at least half a dozen running in production.

Raúl Jiménez

I’m a web developer with 15 years of experience currently working as a front-end architect and consultant in Barcelona. My expertise covers AngularJS, JavaScript, HTML5/CSS3, ActionScript and Media Streaming.
I’m passionate about cross-platform applications, devices and screens and I’m trying to make the web better with my project Videogular.

Pawel Kozlowski

Pawel is software-development addict that believes in free, open source software. He is a core contributor to AngularJS framework, AngularUI, Karma-runner and several other projects. He is the co-author the "Mastering Web Application Development with AngularJS" book. When not coding, Pawel can be spotted speaking at various software development conferences.

Daniel Marjenburgh

I am a Full Stack Developer and Consultant at Xebia who is passionate about building elegant software and delivering great quality. I try to stay on top of things, keep learning new ways of doing and ponder about ways we can make our software better and simpler.

Martin Naumann

Martin is open source contributor and web evangelist by heart from Zurich with a decade experience from the trenches of software engineering in multiple fields.
He works as a software engineer at Archilogic in front- and backend. He devotes his time to moving the web forward, fixing problems, building applications and systems and breaking things for fun & profit.
Martin believes in the web platform and is working with bleeding edge technologies that will allow the web to prosper.

Carmen Popoviciu

Carmen is a front-end engineer and the leading lady of the Dutch AngularJS User Group. She loves Angular and is passionate about its community. She hopes to one day make a difference for the web with the code she writes.

Pascal Precht

Pascal is a front-end engineer with a love for semantic markup and evolving technologies in the open web. He loves contributing to open source and is the creator of the popular angular-translate module.
Pascal loves to travel the world and speaks at conferences and meetups. When he's not busy preparing the next workshop or conference talk, you might find him outside with his skateboard.

Dave Smith

Dave has been developing software professionally for 10 years. He got his start tinkering on a TI-82 calculator in high school and has worked on projects that span the spectrum from embedded devices to user interfaces. Currently, he works at HireVue on the company’s Angular-based web application.
